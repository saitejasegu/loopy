# Privacy Policy — Loopy

Last updated: 2026-07-21

Loopy is a personal habit tracker. This policy describes what data the app handles and how it is used.

## Data Loopy stores

- Habit names, schedules, targets, accent colors, reminder settings, and archive state
- Dated check-in values (including timer and Health-synced totals)
- Optional display name used for the Today greeting
- Appearance preference and onboarding completion flag

This data is stored on your device using SwiftData. When iCloud sync is available and enabled for your Apple ID, habit and check-in data may sync privately through CloudKit to your other devices. Loopy does not operate a public Loopy account server.

## Permissions

- **Notifications:** Used only if you enable habit reminders. You can disable reminders in the app or in iOS Settings.
- **Apple Health:** Used only for Health-backed habits (steps and active energy). Loopy reads those quantities and writes dated local check-ins so streaks work offline. Loopy does not sell Health data.
- **iCloud / CloudKit:** Used for optional private sync of habits and check-ins between your devices.

## Widgets and Shortcuts

Home Screen and Lock Screen widgets, plus App Intents / Shortcuts, read and update the same on-device habit store (via an App Group) so glanceable progress stays consistent with the app.

## What Loopy does not do

- No advertising SDKs
- No third-party analytics in the current build
- No sale of personal data
- No public social profiles

## Contact

For privacy questions about Loopy, contact the developer at the App Store listing support URL for the app.

## App Store privacy nutrition labels (summary)

Disclose as shipped:

- Health & Fitness data (linked to you, used for App Functionality) when HealthKit habits are enabled
- Other User Content / product interaction data stored on device and optionally in private iCloud
- Product Interaction for optional notifications
